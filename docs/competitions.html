ALONE ON THE LINE TOGETHER IN THE STORM

Snowboarding is one of the most solitary things you can do—until competition turns it into something more. Lines aren’t just ridden, they’re witnessed. Shared. Pushed further by the echo of others dropping before and after you. It’s not just you and the mountain—it’s you and everyone who showed up to risk the same silence.

Competitions gather what the mountain usually scatters: riders from all over, driven by hunger, style, and instinct. Different accents. Same stare before the drop. What happens between start gate and bottom isn’t just sport—it’s a shared language made of speed, flight, and fire.

Grizzstorm stands behind this chaos in unity. We don’t just cover events—we follow the convergence. The storm of talent, fear, friendship and fury that turns a comp into something bigger than the sum of its riders. A place where snowboarding speaks for itself. And everyone listens.

THE GRIZZLY THUNDER CUP

The Grizzly Thunder Cup is ours. Born from everything we believe in—focus, flow, and the kind of riding that doesn’t need a crowd to be worth it. No inflated formats. No perfect landings. Just a mountain, a drop, and one clean chance to make it matter.


We’re proud of this because it speaks the way we ride—quiet, direct, and with nothing to prove. It's invite-only, off the map, and shaped by the people who actually ride. Not to win. To be part of something that holds weight.


This isn’t a campaign. It’s a commitment. To the line. To the riders. To the storm.

The Grizzly Thunder Cup will drop into Monte Rosa, Italy—one of the rawest freeride zones in the Alps. Remote, steep, and unforgiving, it’s a place where lines aren’t drawn—they’re earned. No fences. No margin. Just gravity, instinct, and the cold edge of commitment.


Applications are now open. If you think your riding belongs on this face, you’ve got until January 31, 2025 to prove it.

OTHER COMPETITIONS

The Freeride World Tour is more than a contest—it’s a test of instinct. No park features, no perfect landings. Just big faces, fresh snow, and riders choosing their own lines down chaos. Judged on control, creativity and flow, it’s snowboarding at its purest: fast, committed, and unforgiving.


Grizzstorm covers the Tour for what it really is—a gathering of riders who drop in with intention. Every run is a decision. Every result is earned.

The FIS Snowboard World Cup is where structure meets speed. Organized, global, and fiercely competitive, it brings together top riders to race gates, hit features, and land tricks with precision. The stakes are high, the runs are clean, and the margin for error is razor-thin.


Grizzstorm tracks the Cup not for medals, but for moments—the quiet pressure before the start, the split-second adjustments mid-air, the poise under rules. This is snowboarding with discipline, drive, and a hunger to master the format.

The X Games are snowboarding turned up to 11—flash, risk, and pure spectacle. With towering jumps, flawless style, and a crowd roaring on impact, it’s where progression lives. Tricks land here that reshape the sport. Lines are clean, grabs are locked, and everything’s on the edge of impossible.


Grizzstorm watches the X Games for the fire. For the commitment it takes to go bigger every run, and the focus behind the chaos. Style isn’t optional here—it’s the currency. And every rider’s out to rewrite the standard.

It’s something you survive, shape, and leave behind—one clean line at a time.

We track what matters. The kind of events that move the needle.
