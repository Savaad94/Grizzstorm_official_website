Meet the

ATHLETES

WHO RIDE WITH US

We don’t hand out stickers—we back riders who move the needle. Sponsorship isn’t about trends or followers. It’s about intent, impact, and progression.

Grizzstorm supports athletes who bring raw energy, vision, and purpose to the mountain.
The ones who ride first and talk later.

These are the riders shaping snowboarding. This is who we ride with.

KOKOMO MURASE

Kokomo Murase rides with the kind of focus and intensity that defines Grizzstorm. From her early days in Japan to claiming podiums on the world stage, she’s pushed snowboarding with quiet determination and bold style.


We don’t chase names—we follow intention. Kokomo’s riding speaks louder than hype, and that’s exactly why she’s part of the Grizzstorm crew.


Progression isn’t just a goal—it’s her default setting.


ZEB POWELL

Zeb Powell doesn’t follow lines—he bends them. His riding is explosive, creative, and completely his own, turning every run into something unpredictable and unforgettable.


At Grizzstorm, we back riders who bring energy with purpose. Zeb’s style isn’t just flair—it’s control, power, and intent.


He’s not here to fit in. He’s here to shift the whole damn vibe. That’s exactly why he rides with us.


THOSE WHO RIDES

<

LUCAS EGUIBAR

>

Lucas Eguibar rides with speed, precision, and grit. As a world champion and Olympic finalist, he’s put Spanish snowboarding on the map — one gate at a time. His edge control and tactical vision make him one of the most respected racers on the Snowboard Cross circuit.


In a sport where every millisecond counts, Lucas brings order to chaos. He doesn’t just react — he anticipates. Whether flying over rollers or charging through traffic, his riding reflects composure under pressure and a fierce drive to win.


We believe in athletes who carve their own path. Lucas doesn’t follow lines — he defines them. Every race is a statement, and every turn is earned. He’s not just fast — he’s fearless.


<

CHLOE KIM

>

Chloe Kim is a global icon of snowboarding. A two-time Olympic gold medalist, she redefined the halfpipe with massive amplitude and creative runs. Her fearless approach and confident style have changed what’s expected in women’s competition — and she’s still pushing boundaries.


From her teen debut to world domination, Chloe has always stood out. Her riding combines flair, precision, and pressure-proof nerves. She executes with calmness in chaos, turning each descent into a signature performance.


Chloe doesn't just compete — she creates. With every grab, spin, and landing, she’s building her legacy. She’s not riding in anyone’s tracks. She’s carving her own story into snowboarding history.


<

HIROTO OGIWARA

>

Hiroto Ogiwara represents the future of Japanese snowboarding. With quiet confidence and razor-sharp technique, he takes on every feature with calculated intent. From backcountry lines to competition rails, Hiroto rides with control, flow, and finesse.


What sets him apart is his ability to make complex tricks look effortless. Every movement is smooth, every choice deliberate. He’s not just learning — he’s evolving the sport.


Hiroto rides like a craftsman — deliberate, precise, and powerful. He doesn’t shout for attention. His riding does that for him. And the message is clear: Japan’s next snowboarding era is here.


<

PATRICK HOFMANN

>

Patrick Hoffmann brings a unique blend of Swiss precision and creative energy. His lines are bold, his style unmistakable, and every session is a statement. He thrives where control meets chaos, making technical riding look natural.


From big mountain freeride to progressive park tricks, Patrick’s versatility is unmatched. He attacks each run with intent and style, adapting fluidly to whatever terrain lies ahead.


Snowboarding, for Patrick, is more than sport — it’s expression through movement. Every grab and turn is calculated art. He doesn’t just ride the mountain — he draws on it.


<

LUDOVIC GUILLOT-DIAT

>

Ludovic Guillot-Diat is a veteran of the big lines. With decades in the game and roots in the French Alps, he’s a freerider whose style is built on experience, instinct, and raw mountain power.


He rides with flow, control, and commitment, dropping into zones where others hesitate. His descents are confident and clean, shaped by a lifetime of knowing snow, terrain, and timing.


Ludo isn’t chasing fame — he’s chasing perfect lines. With quiet leadership and fearless execution, he inspires the next wave of freeriders. He is what riding with soul looks like.


<

MIA BROOKES

>

Mia Brookes is the most exciting young talent in snowboarding today. At just 16, she landed the first 1440 in women’s history — and that was just the start. Mia’s approach is fast, fearless, and fluid.


She rides with the joy of discovery and the confidence of a veteran. Her lines are spontaneous but sharp, and every trick feels uniquely hers.


Mia isn’t waiting for the future. She’s defining it right now — turning heads, breaking records, and proving that the next chapter in women’s snowboarding is already underway.


<

NURIA CASTAN BARAN

>

Núria Castán Barón is one of Europe’s premier freeriders. With deep roots in Spain’s mountains and a fearless spirit, she rides steep, technical terrain with style and control.


Her lines are aggressive yet elegant, blending power and grace in high-stakes backcountry environments. Núria reads the mountain like a map and paints it with movement.


She’s a leader in women’s freeride, showing that beauty and brutality can coexist on a snowboard. When Núria drops, she makes a statement — not just about skill, but about courage.


<

VICTOR DE LE RUE

>

Victor de Le Rue is snowboarding’s wildest technician. Born in the French Pyrenees, he’s built a reputation for attacking huge lines with speed, control, and imagination.


His descents are both powerful and poetic — massive cliff drops, fast spins, and impossible angles, all executed with methodical precision. He brings chaos under command.


Victor doesn’t follow rules. He rewrites them with every ride. From contest wins to film segments, his riding is a masterclass in risk, talent, and pure love for the mountain.


<

WANG ZIYANG

>

Wang Ziyang is leading a snowboarding revolution in China. With a mix of technical consistency and creative style, he’s become a standout presence in Asian and global scenes alike.


Whether in slopestyle or street, Wang delivers clean tricks and confident landings. His quiet presence masks a deep intensity — and he brings it to every session.


He’s not here just to compete. He’s here to inspire — to open doors for future riders across Asia and push snowboarding to new places, literally and figuratively.


<

DUSTY HENRICKSEN

>

Dusty Henricksen exploded onto the scene with unfiltered energy and creativity. He’s bold, unpredictable, and electric on a snowboard — the kind of rider you can’t take your eyes off.


From streets to slopestyle, Dusty throws tricks most wouldn’t even consider. He lands with swagger and rides with joy, always pushing limits but never losing his spark.


Dusty is the spirit of progression — wild, technical, and fearless. He’s not copying anyone. He’s inventing new ways to ride, one stomped landing at a time.


HALL OF FAME

TRAVIS RICE

Travis Rice doesn’t compete with other riders—he competes with the mountain.


Backcountry visionary, film pioneer, and founder of Natural Selection, he made snowboarding feel like art and war at once.


He dropped lines no one else would touch and captured them with a lens as sharp as his edges.


Progression doesn’t follow Travis—it chases him.


SHAUN WHITE

Shaun White didn’t just dominate the halfpipe—he turned it into a global stage.


With three Olympic golds and a career that redefined what was possible on a snowboard, he became the face of progression and pressure.


His riding was precision, fire, and control under the spotlight.


He didn’t ride trends—he created them.


JAMIE ANDERSON

Jamie Anderson rides like she belongs there—because she does.


Fluid, powerful, consistent. With Olympic and X Games golds stacked across more than a decade, she made slopestyle her domain.


But it’s not just about medals—it’s how she makes it look effortless. Her riding is intention in motion.


That’s what a queen looks like in snow.
